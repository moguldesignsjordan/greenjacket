# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local Mansfield/DFW-area visitors browsing on phone or desktop: golfers booking a simulator bay, families planning birthdays or weekend outings, couples looking for a date-night spot, corporate organizers scoping team events, and league players checking schedules. The audience is broad and local, including older guests, so the site needs to read clearly at a glance, not just look good. The job to be done on any given page is almost always "decide, then act" — book a bay, join, book a lesson, or send an inquiry.

## Product Purpose

The website is The Jacket's primary conversion engine: drive simulator bay bookings (Yourgolfbooking), membership signups (Joinit), lesson and club-fitting bookings (Square), and event/private-party inquiries. It also serves as the digital storefront — communicating location, hours, food & drink, social proof (Google reviews), and FAQs. Success looks like visitors quickly understanding what The Jacket is, feeling welcomed rather than sold to, and converting into a booking, member, or inquiry with minimal friction.

## Brand Personality

"Upscale tech meets laid-back bar." Premium Trackman iO simulator technology paired with relaxed, social, neighborhood sports-bar hospitality. Confident about the tech without being clinical or corporate about it — explain accuracy and quality in plain, warm language a friend would use, not a spec sheet. The voice is a local spot that happens to have best-in-class gear, not an enterprise SaaS pitch.

## Anti-references

- **Big-box entertainment chains (Topgolf-style)** — avoid mega-brand, theme-park energy, neon-drenched arcade vibes, or anything that feels like a national franchise. The Jacket is a neighborhood spot.
- **Generic golf/SaaS tech sites** — cold, corporate, jargon-heavy. The current placeholder copy ("structural", "telemetry", "kinetic", "operational", "deploy", "matrix") is a holdover from this anti-pattern and needs a full rewrite to a warm, conversational, locally-grounded voice.
- **Cluttered sports-bar visual noise** — wall-to-wall TV walls, beer-brand logo soup, busy layouts. Keep it clean and confident even where it's social.

## Design Principles

1. **Local-first warmth** — every page should read like an invitation from a neighborhood spot, not a corporate vendor or franchise.
2. **Tech credibility without jargon** — lean on Trackman iO accuracy as a real differentiator, but explain it the way a friend who works there would.
3. **One clear next action per page** — booking, membership, lessons, or an inquiry; never bury or compete CTAs against each other.
4. **Show, don't oversell** — real reviews, real hours, real address, real pricing. Let specifics and social proof build trust over abstract claims.
5. **Readable for everyone, first** — the broad local/family audience (including older guests) should be able to read body copy and CTAs comfortably without squinting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline with extra emphasis on readability for a broad-age, family/local audience: body text contrast comfortably above 4.5:1 (avoid light-gray-on-white for anything important), avoid undersized type, keep tap targets generous, and ensure all imagery (hero video, icons) has appropriate alt/labels and respects reduced-motion preferences.
